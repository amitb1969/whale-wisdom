Our partnership delivered a gain of +44.8% net of all fees, expenses, and allocations for the full year 2025.
What would have already been considered a satisfactory year through September turned into a stellar year,
thanks to our partnership’s performance in the fourth quarter.
While we are quite happy with the result, we would like to be clear that years like 2025 are not something
we expect to repeat with any regularity. Volatility is often an admission price for pursuing exceptional long-
term returns, and we expect there will inevitably be periods where our performance looks very different
than it did this year. Ultimately, the only yardstick that matters to us is our long -term CAGR, and we are
pleased with the rate of compounding that we have delivered to our partners since inception.
Our fourth quarter gains were driven primarily by our investment in D-BOX Technologies (“D-BOX”), a
Canadian-based micro-cap company which became a material holding during the second half of 2025.7 In
December 2025, we shared an investment memorandum detailing our thoughts on D-BOX, which can be
found here for anyone interested. D-BOX is a position we continue to own with conviction.
We have long maintained that finding just one or two exceptional ideas each year , and sizing them
appropriately, should be sufficient to enable us to generate satisfactory returns. We were fortunate to find
and build significant positions in two companies last year, Dadelo and D-BOX, each of which experienced
a triple-digit percentage share price gain in 2025 , and together accounted for a meaningful share of our
performance.7 The global investible universe remains vast, and with some luck, we hope and expect to
continue to find what we believe to be asymmetric opportunities that contribute to our future performance.
Equally important is limiting mistakes. Last year, we believe we did a decent job on that front, as measured
by the frequency and magnitude of our position-level losses. I am far from immune from making mistakes,
however, and have made plenty over the years. Near year-end, for only the second time since Sohra Peak’s
founding (which has not been often enough), I thoroughly reviewed every partnership investment since
inception that resulted in a loss. For every loss that reflected a mistake rather than bad luck, I wanted to try
and understand exactly where my thinking went wrong, and what I could have done differently.
The review was humbling, but also clarifying. I found that many of the same mistake patterns repeated
themselves far more often than I imagined . While it is never fun to re -live the decisions that cost money,
I’ve come to appreciate this as an invaluable exercise, as every investor is sure to carry a set of blind spots.
What seems to matter is whether we are willing to address them, with intellectual honesty, and do what we
can to reduce them. To that end, I plan to revisit this post-mortem regularly and scrutinize future mistakes
more frequently, and as a result, hope to make better decisions and incur fewer avoidable losses over time.
Approaching New Subscription Limit
At the moment, we are open to accepting an additional $3 million in new subscriptions. Once our threshold
is reached, we intend to close the partnership to new subscriptions forever.  For much of the past year, we
have anticipated reaching a point when we would intend to stop accepting new capital into our partnership.
It appears that point may be soon approaching.
As expressed, we desire to always maintain a capital base small and nimble enough that allows us to invest
in our highest conviction ideas with sufficient concentration. Our goal remains focused on responsibly
maximizing investment returns rather than maximizing assets under management. The way we view it, this
approach mitigates risk to the durability of our future returns on capital, just as we would expect our

Transitioning to Semi-Annual Letter Cadence
A year ago, I shared my thoughts regarding how at some point in the future, I would plan to transition our
investor letters from a quarterly to a semi -annual cadence. I have made the decision that, following this
letter, our partnership’s letters will transition to being written and shared at a semi-annual cadence.
As much as I genuinely enjoy writing to each of you with updates on our partnership’s operations, writing
thoughtful updates has proven to require significant time, and comes with a meaningful opportunity  cost,
time that could be spent focused on our investments. Additionally, I’ve long believed that semi -annual
letters strike the right balance between providing our partners with timely updates and aligning the
frequency of our updates with our long-term investment horizon. As a result, I hope that this transition will
be incrementally beneficial for our partnership.
Administratively, everything else will remain unchanged . All limited partners will continue to receive
monthly capital account statements from our fund administrator. Should any partners wish to chat at more
frequent intervals, please know that my door is always open. I’d be happy to hear from you.
